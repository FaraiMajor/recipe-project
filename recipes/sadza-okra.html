Sadza and Okra recipe

INGEDIENTS

  • 1½ – 2 cups chopped okra
  • 1 frsh tomato
  • 2 birds eye chilli
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon bicarbonate of soda

DESCRIPTION

I have got very fond memories of my grandmother cooking okra for us. Whenever she cooked okra we knew that we would need to cook so much sadza (maize meal or pap) that day because this yummy vegetable calls for a lot of sadza.
This special Zimbabwean delicacy, the Okra, also known Ladies’ Fingers, Derere [Zimbabwe] and Delele [Zambia]. An ecellent source of vitamin C, with zero fat, this Okra soup recipe will have your taste buds dancing. Goes together well when coupled with Sadza. Im getting Hungry already...

Cook Time: 20 minutes Serving: 3-4 people

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Wash and slice the okra into equally thin slices, remove the stems and the tips. Chop the onion and tomatoes and set them aside.
  2. Pour water and bring to boil
  3. Once boiled, add bicarbonated of soda and stir in to mix
  4. Add chopped okra and birds eye chilli
  5. Leave to boil for about 10 minutes whilst stirring
  6. Add fresh tomatoes, salt and stir in. Leave to cook for another 5 minutes
  7. Leave to simmer for another 5 mintes then serve
