Matemba/Kapenta

INGEDIENTS

  • 1 cup dried kapenta
  • 2 tbs cooking oil
  • 2 large tomato
  • 1/2 black pepper
  • 1 spring onion or shallot
  • 1/2 white onion (diced)
  • 1 tsp salt

DESCRIPTION

It is known as kapenta, a fish of Lake Tanganyika, although it is also frequently found in the Zambezi River, which extends its waters between Zambia and Zimbabwe. It is a very small fish which is eaten as a snack while walking through the streets of the city. It can also be included in sauces and served with rice and sadza. The very small fish usually eaten dry because of their large quantities are somewhat very affordable. Middle-income families in Zimbabwe ate them more. Even in rural areas, they were eaten so much because back then there were no fridges so the dried meals were very convenient.

Cook Time: 60 minutes Serving: 6 people

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Soak the kapenta in cold water for 5 minutes before cooking them. Meanwhile, you can be dicing the onions and grating tomatoes.
  2. In a pan add the cooking oil, when it heats add the onions , fry until they are caramelized.
  3. Now you can add the kapenta, fry together for 8 minutes then add the grated tomatoes, close the lid and cook for 5 minutes or until the tomatoes are fully cooked.
  4. Add some salt (if needed) and ground black pepper. Sprinkle some freshly chopped spring onions or parsley
